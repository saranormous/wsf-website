Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about WSF and giving to Woodside School

WSF funds a wide range of programs that would otherwise be cut from the school budget. These include:

  • Art
  • Music — Instrumental and Vocal
  • Physical Education
  • Early Literacy and Reading Discovery
  • Spanish language instruction
  • Mandarin language instruction
  • Science
  • Performing Arts
  • Outdoor Education
  • Technology
  • Staff Development
  • Curriculum Development
  • Librarian services
  • Middle School Development
  • Global Citizen Curriculum

Without WSF, these programs would be cut entirely and class sizes would increase significantly.

Woodside Elementary School costs approximately $14 million per year to operate — roughly $37,000 per student.

Public funding through property taxes covers approximately $12 million of that total. WSF fills the remaining gap of approximately $2.25 million each year, ensuring that all the programs our students and families value can continue.

WSF's suggested contribution is $5,500 per enrolled student. This represents the difference between what the state provides and what it actually costs to deliver a Woodside education.

That said, every gift at any amount matters. Even if you can't give the full per-student ask, your contribution still counts toward our 100% participation goal — a milestone celebrated by all students with an ice cream truck party!

WSF raises funds through four main channels:

  1. Annual Fund — Our primary fundraising campaign, held each fall. Families are asked to make a direct gift to support the school's programs.
  2. Grand Auction — An annual spring gala and auction that has been a beloved Woodside tradition since 1984. It features live and silent auction items, a dinner, and community celebration.
  3. Community Partners — Local businesses sponsor WSF programs and events throughout the year, connecting our school with the broader Woodside community.
  4. Capital Campaigns — When a major facility or program investment is needed, WSF launches targeted capital campaigns. Past examples include the Design Lab, the Sellman Auditorium, and the Preschool building.
